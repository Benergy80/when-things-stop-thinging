The Timeline of Dissolution

1968

The White Album

A pure white object containing four portraits, glossy photos, and the weight of cultural history. The objecthood itself was a statement—so much contained within so little shown.

1973

Dark Side of the Moon

A prism splitting light into infinite colors, the journey contained in physical grooves. The cover art promised a transformation, and the vinyl delivered it.

1999

Napster Arrives

The first crack in the dam. Music becomes files, sharing becomes downloading, ownership becomes access. The beginning of the end.

2008

Class at Art Institute

The moment of realization: when things stop thinging, we lose more than objects. We lose the containers of meaning, the anchors of memory.

2024

The End of Permanence

Cultural touchstones dissolve into the digital stream. No more shared moments, no more common references, just algorithmic feeds of personalized nothing.
